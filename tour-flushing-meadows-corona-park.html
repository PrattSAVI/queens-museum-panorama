Flushing Meadows Corona Park

Welcome to Flushing Meadows Corona Park!

In Flushing Meadows Corona Park, numerous structures remain from the 1964-1965 New York World's Fair including: the Unisphere, the New York State Pavilion, the Port Authority Heliport (now the Terrace on the Park restaurant), the New York Hall of Science, and the New York City Building (now the Queens Museum). The New York State Marine Amphitheater, demolished in 1996, remains on the map at the edge of Meadow Lake. The Queens Zoo, opposite the Queens Museum over the Grand Central Parkway, was erected in 1968 to occupy the central lawns of the former Transportation Zone. Citi Field and the USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center are also depicted nearby the Mets-Willets Point MTA subway station.
